ASEAN Economic & Demographic Overview

Interactive Power BI dashboard providing a comparative overview of ASEAN countries from 2017 to 2022. It analyzes trade flows (exports, imports, total trade), GDP and growth, GDP per capita, population indicators, and tourism arrivals, allowing users to explore trends over time and differences across countries. The dashboard is designed to support policy analysis, economic comparison, and regional trend monitoring, with clear visual structure, consistent metrics, and intuitive country-level breakdowns.

Sales Breakdown

This Power BI dashboard provides a Sales Breakdown analysis, showcasing key metrics such as total customers, orders, and revenue. It includes visualizations of credit limits vs. revenue by customers, quantity ordered by product categories, and order trends by year, quarter, and month. Additionally, it analyzes order distribution across offices and countries, helping to monitor sales performance and identify trends effectively.

Data Professional Survey Breakdown

This Power BI dashboard provides a Data Professional Survey Breakdown, analyzing responses from 630 survey takers with an average age of 29.87. It includes insights into the countries of survey participants, gender distribution, and favorite programming languages, where Python dominates. The dashboard also presents average salaries by job title, highlighting Data Scientists and Data Engineers as top earners. Additionally, it features happiness scores, with work-life balance rated 5.86 and salary satisfaction at 4.27, offering a comprehensive overview of the data profession landscape.

Course Participation and Completion Rates

This Power BI dashboard presents an overview of course participation and completion rates across three EU-related training courses: Social Security Coordination – Basic, Posting of Workers – Basic, and Road Transport – Basic. The dashboard compares the number of participants who completed each course versus those who did not, highlighting differences in engagement and completion patterns. With a total of 794 participants, the analysis shows that Social Security Coordination – Basic has the highest overall participation, while Road Transport – Basic records the lowest. This dashboard is designed to support monitoring, evaluation, and reporting of training activities, enabling stakeholders to quickly assess participation levels and completion outcomes across courses.
